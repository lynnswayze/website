How I would create a full pipeline of proactive clients

February 22, 2026

One of the biggest problems in family law right now is that the entire family law paradigm is built on an outdated house of cards:

  • Hourly billing and the retainer/escrow model
  • Selling tactics (motions, documents) over solutions
  • Reactive conflict rather than proactive peace-making
These three elements, combined with a high cost of entry into the field, suppressed wages, high cost of living, unaddressed mental health issues, and other causes a lot of pain for attorneys, for families, and for communities.

I can't solve for the systemic problems, but were I a family lawyer, here's how I'd create a network of proactive clients I'd love working with:

  • Step 1: Go out to the community (library, churches, clubs, etc) and educate families on what happens when they don't know their rights, responsibilities, and options ... and paint a new picture for them that's peacemaking and collaborative (unbundled, mediated, etc), rather than reactive and rooted in fear (e.g., litigation)
  • Step 2: Get these families -- ideally BEFORE they divorce or have conflict -- into workshops or consultations where they build out their family plan for what they want for their family long-term, as well as commitments between them to remain civil should things go sour. (Remember, 50%+ of couples split. Why does that have to mean litigation?)
  • Step 3: Follow up with these families at least once a year to get them to update their plan based on what's new... and remind them of new laws, changes, and of their commitment to peace.
  • Step 4: Have a plan/offer for unbundled or limited scope work, subject to local jurisdictional rules. If possible, I'd have a plan for trial so that I, or another attorney, can take on those cases, referring out to avoid conflicts of interest but only to someone who "gets it" and is as committed to peacemaking as I am.
