What's better than a fancy website?

February 21, 2026

So many family law attorneys are worried about having a fancy website.

(Check out examples of "fancy" sites I've built here, here, and here)

And don't get me wrong: a nice website feels good and has its place, but it's not more important than:

  • Speaking directly to who you serve best, using language that's actually relatable. Whether your best clients are moms who are child-focused, military vets, or those with special needs... you'll catch far more clients with language that speaks to them, rather than generic SEO language that doesn't.

  • Giving an easy way to get a hold of you that isn't a "contact me form." Even better, an Info Kit which presells you before they ever speak with you

  • Providing, throughout your website, answers to common questions (in an FAQ, your blog, videos, etc) with real answers that actually represent what it will be like to work with you

Your website's job is to give your best clients the ability to see themselves working with you and to make that process easy... and everyone else should be able to self-select themselves out of working with you.

To do anything less is just wasting everyone's time...especially yours. So don't get hung up on how it looks until you have a plan for addressing the above.
