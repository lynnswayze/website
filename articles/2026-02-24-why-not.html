Why not?

February 24, 2026

Today's thought experiment:

What would happen if you only worked with the types of clients you absolutely love?

I'm serious. Family law is so critically underserved by attorneys that you could stand up as the only person who works with military families, or LGBTQ couples, or whatever... and if you have a compelling story and reason why, and a great service delivery... you could fill your pipeline really quickly.

And you know what else happens when you can niche down like this?

You'll:

  • Fill your testimonials and case studies will people who say things like, "This lawyer truly gets me!" and "I hired this lawyer because they had lots of experience solving this problem... they were a life saver!" and "I have told all my friends like me about this lawyer."

  • Start getting referrals from this type of client, so that sales becomes easier and more profitable because not only are the leads warm, the proof you have to underserved all match the prospect exactly

  • Get really good at solving problems for this type of client, which helps with the aforementioned proof, but also you'll get really good at knowing what to say and how to say it to help your clients feel heard and supported

  • Feel more comfortable referring out clients who aren't a fit, generating goodwill with fellow attorneys and, likely, referrals back to you
There is no reason you have to take everyone who walks in the door, especially if you're doing marketing correctly.

So I gotta ask... why not just go for it?

- Lynn
